## Deep-slot decision

The two completed exemplar studies have already been published in the committed
library as `dropout-pressure-law-streaming-language-models` and
`budgeted-guardrails-mlp-channel-interventions`. Reusing either would repackage
published evidence and violate the originality rule.

The unused deterministic agent projects are labeled fixtures or controlled
replays with small authored datasets and policy outputs determined by encoded
rules. They do not provide independent observations, multiple seeds or genuine
repeats, uncertainty estimates, blinded labels, or an evidence path strong
enough for a consequential empirical claim. Several also repeat the library's
heavily covered release-gate, authorization, routing, provenance, and agent-risk
mechanisms.

The strongest unused candidate remains `heading-aware-retrieval`, but it still
cannot honestly score methodological rigor 4/5:

- twelve queries were authored from known target articles;
- five perturbations per query are correlated variants, not independent
  information needs;
- relevance is inferred from the authored target rather than blinded judgments;
- the corpus contains only the 46 committed articles from one publication;
- there is no semantic-retrieval baseline or heading-removal/shuffled-heading
  negative control;
- the heading-aware hypothesis was falsified by the saved result: fixed windows
  achieved MRR 0.889 versus 0.816 for heading-enriched paragraphs;
- the evidence manifest references a figure that is not present in the project.

The saved run is useful exploratory evidence and should remain internal. It does
not support promotion to a publication-grade deep-research article without a
new, independently judged and better-controlled study.
