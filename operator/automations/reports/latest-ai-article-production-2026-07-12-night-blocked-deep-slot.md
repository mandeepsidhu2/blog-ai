## Deep-slot evidence audit

The retrieval run reports fixed overlapping windows at MRR 0.889 and hit@5
1.000, heading-enriched paragraphs at MRR 0.816 and hit@5 0.983, and plain
paragraphs at MRR 0.799 and hit@5 0.983. The directional hypothesis is
falsified, which is publishable in principle, but the current design does not
support a publication-grade comparative claim:

1. All 12 information needs were authored from known target articles, making
   target leakage and evaluator expectancy plausible.
2. The five reported repeats are fixed string transformations of each original
   query, not independent information needs or independent judgments.
3. Relevance is a single preassigned target slug. There are no blinded,
   multi-judge, or graded relevance labels and no inter-rater agreement.
4. The experiment uses one small, topically clustered 46-document corpus, so
   uncertainty across queries cannot establish corpus-level generality.
5. Controls do not include a semantic retriever, randomized heading text, or a
   heading-removal ablation that isolates the mechanism attributed to headings.
6. The evidence manifest lists `heading-aware-retrieval.svg`, but that artifact
   does not exist. The deep slot therefore also fails the manifest and real
   result-visual requirements.

The weakest potential public claim would be that fixed windows generally
outperform heading-aware chunking. The saved evidence supports only the much
narrower observation that fixed windows ranked the authored targets higher in
this particular lexical test. That narrow result is not consequential enough
for the required deep-research slot without stronger independent evaluation.
