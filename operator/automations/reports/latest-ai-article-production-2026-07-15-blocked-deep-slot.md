## Deep-slot evidence audit

The retrieval screen reports fixed windows at MRR 0.889 and hit@5 1.000,
heading-enriched paragraphs at MRR 0.816 and hit@5 0.983, and plain paragraphs
at MRR 0.799 and hit@5 0.983. Its directional hypothesis was falsified, but the
design still cannot justify a publication-grade comparative claim:

1. All 12 information needs were authored from known target articles, so target
   leakage and evaluator expectancy are unresolved.
2. The five reported repeats are deterministic rewrites of each query, not
   independent information needs, judgments, samples, or stochastic runs.
3. Relevance is a single preassigned target slug. There are no blinded graded
   judgments, multiple raters, or agreement estimates.
4. The corpus is one topically clustered article library and has not been
   replicated on a held-out corpus or prespecified temporal split.
5. There is no semantic-retrieval baseline, heading-removal ablation, or
   randomized-heading control to isolate heading context from chunk length and
   lexical duplication.
6. The manifest lists `heading-aware-retrieval.svg`, but that artifact still
   does not exist; therefore the manifest and evidence-visual requirements
   cannot pass.
7. The public library has grown from 46 to 52 documents since the saved run,
   but the results were not regenerated. Rerunning the same target-derived
   design would not repair its causal or external-validity limitations.

The weakest prospective claim is that fixed windows generally outperform
heading-aware chunks. The artifacts support only the narrow observation that
fixed windows ranked the authored target slugs higher in one lexical screen.
That observation is not enough to teach a senior engineer a trustworthy general
decision rule.
