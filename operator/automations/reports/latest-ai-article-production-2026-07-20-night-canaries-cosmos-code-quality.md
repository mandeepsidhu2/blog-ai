## Deep evidence project

Project: `operator/diy-project-blogs/projects/risk-weighted-canary-sampling`

- 400-case budget; population shares 92% routine, 7% important, 1% critical.
- Three allocations: population proportional, 55/25/20 risk weighted, and
  balanced strata.
- Four scenarios: heterogeneous confirmatory, homogeneous, deliberately
  misranked risk, and no-effect null.
- 4,000 repeats per scenario-policy cell, 48,000 saved repeat rows total, and
  5,000 repeat-level bootstrap resamples for descriptive intervals.
- Population sampling observed 4.00 critical cases and detected the focal
  regression in 0.25% of repeats (95% interval 0.10%–0.43%).
- Risk weighting observed 79.99 critical cases and detected it in 97.70%
  (97.22%–98.15%). Balanced sampling reached 99.95% but reduced effective
  population sample size to 156.8 versus 256.4 for risk weighting and 400.0
  for population sampling.
- The risk-weighted inverse-probability estimate had +0.016 percentage-point
  mean bias; its unweighted estimate had +3.986 points of bias.
- The misranked-risk control reduced nominal critical detection to 0.15%, so
  the public claim explicitly does not treat risk labels as ground truth.
- The version-1 evidence manifest traces configuration, runner, 48,000-row CSV,
  aggregates, statistical declaration, focal output, and generated result
  figure.

No Torch, CPU-Torch, CUDA, MPS experiment, provider inference, LM Studio model,
AWS, Terraform, Tofu, S3, deployment, or cloud-resource command ran.
