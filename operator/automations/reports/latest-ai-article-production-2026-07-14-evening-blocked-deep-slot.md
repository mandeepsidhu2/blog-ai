## Deep-slot evidence audit

The retrieval screen reports fixed overlapping windows at MRR 0.889 and hit@5
1.000, heading-enriched paragraphs at MRR 0.816 and hit@5 0.983, and plain
paragraphs at MRR 0.799 and hit@5 0.983. The directional hypothesis was
falsified, but the design remains inadequate for a consequential comparative
claim:

1. The 12 information needs were authored from known target articles, leaving
   target leakage and evaluator expectancy unresolved.
2. The five repeats are deterministic string transformations, not independent
   information needs, judgments, or runs.
3. Relevance is one preassigned target slug; there are no blinded graded
   judgments, multiple raters, or agreement measurements.
4. The experiment uses one topically clustered 46-document corpus and provides
   no held-out corpus replication.
5. It lacks a semantic retriever baseline, a heading-removal ablation, and a
   randomized-heading control capable of isolating the proposed mechanism.
6. The manifest still lists `heading-aware-retrieval.svg`, which does not
   exist, so the required artifact-generated result visual and manifest checks
   cannot pass.

The weakest potential public claim is that fixed windows generally outperform
heading-aware chunking. These artifacts support only the narrower observation
that fixed windows ranked authored targets higher in this one lexical screen.
That result is not sufficient for the required deep-research slot.
